    How We Calculate Your Match
    A transparent account of our scoring formula

    1 — You select topics and set your positions

    Choose up to 10 policy areas. For each, you pick a direction and a strength from 1 (slight preference) to 5 (extremely strongly held). The labels for each direction are specific to the topic — for example, on NHS funding the choice is "Increase investment / Keep as is / Reduce & reform" rather than a generic "Support / Neutral / Oppose". Neutral direction contributes zero to your score regardless of your strength, which lets you include topics you're undecided on without skewing results.

    USS = User Strength (1–5) Direction → Sign: Left option = +1, Middle = 0, Right option = −1

    2 — Party positions are scored from their manifesto

    Each party's position on every topic is rated 1–5 based on their published manifesto. Where a manifesto has not yet been published, scores are estimated from existing public policy positions and clearly marked as estimated. Scores are derived from official manifesto text; all scoring decisions are documented in the party data files.

    PS = Party Score (1–5) 5 = Strongly champions — a flagship commitment 4 = Supportive — included in their programme 3 = Neutral — no clear position or not mentioned 2 = Mild opposition — sceptical or would scale back 1 = Strongly opposed — would reverse or block

    3 — Match contribution calculated per topic

    For each topic, we calculate how well your position aligns with the party's position. The formula converts the party's 1–5 score into a −2 to +2 range by subtracting 3. This means a party that strongly supports an issue (+2) and a user who supports it produce a large positive contribution; a mismatch produces a negative contribution. The user's strength multiplies the contribution, so topics you care deeply about count more.

    MC (Match Contribution) = USS × (PS − 3) × UserSign Example — you strongly support NHS investment (USS=5, Sign=+1): Party A scores 5 on NHS: MC = 5 × (5−3) × 1 = +10 ✓ strong match Party B scores 1 on NHS: MC = 5 × (1−3) × 1 = −10 ✗ mismatch Example — you strongly oppose independence (USS=5, Sign=−1): Party C scores 5 on independence: MC = 5 × (5−3) × −1 = −10 ✗ mismatch Party D scores 1 on independence: MC = 5 × (1−3) × −1 = +10 ✓ strong match

    4 — Scores normalised to 0–100

    Raw Party Match Scores (PMS) are summed across all your selected topics. Because raw scores can be negative or span a wide range depending on how many topics you chose and how strongly you feel, we normalise them so the best-matching party always scores 100% and the worst-matching always scores 0%, with others sitting proportionally between them.

    PMS = Σ MC (sum of all match contributions across your selected topics) Normalised = (PMS − min) ÷ (max − min) × 100

    Limitations & transparency

    Party scores involve judgement calls when interpreting manifesto text. Where a manifesto has not been published, scores are estimated — this is flagged clearly on every result card. All party data files are publicly available in the site's GitHub repository. This tool is non-partisan; all parties are scored using the same scale and methodology. It is for informational purposes only and does not constitute voting advice.
