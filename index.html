How We Calculate Your Match
A transparent account of our scoring formula

1 — You select topics and set your positions

Choose up to 10 policy areas. For each, you pick a direction (Support / Neutral / Oppose) and a strength from 1 (slight) to 5 (extremely strong). Neutral direction contributes zero to your score — this lets you include topics you're undecided on without skewing results.

USS = User Strength (1–5) User direction → Sign: Support = +1, Neutral = 0, Oppose = −1

2 — Party positions are scored

Each party's manifesto position on every topic is rated on a scale of 1–5, where 5 = strongly supports, 3 = neutral / no clear position, and 1 = strongly opposes. Scores are derived from official manifesto text; parties without a published manifesto are estimated from existing policy positions and are marked clearly.

PS = Party Score (1–5) 5 = Strongly champions 4 = Supportive 3 = Neutral 2 = Mild opposition 1 = Strongly opposes

3 — Match contribution calculated per topic

For each topic, we calculate how well your position aligns with the party's position. The formula uses the effective party position (PS − 3), so a party that strongly supports an issue (+2) and a user who supports it multiply to give a large positive contribution; a mismatch gives a negative contribution.

MC (Match Contribution) = USS × (PS − 3) × UserSign Example: You strongly support NHS funding (USS=5, Sign=+1) Party A scores 5 on NHS: MC = 5 × (5−3) × 1 = +10 ✓ strong match Party B scores 1 on NHS: MC = 5 × (1−3) × 1 = −10 ✗ mismatch

4 — Scores normalised to 0–100

Raw Party Match Scores (PMS = sum of all MC values) can be negative. We normalise across all parties so the best match scores 100 and the worst scores 0, giving you a clear relative ranking.

PMS = Σ MC (sum across all selected topics) Normalised = (PMS − min) ÷ (max − min) × 100

Limitations & transparency

Party scores are derived from manifesto text and involve judgement calls. Parties without a published 2026 manifesto are estimated from existing positions — this is flagged on every result. This tool is non-partisan; all parties are scored using the same methodology. It is for informational purposes only and does not constitute voting advice.
